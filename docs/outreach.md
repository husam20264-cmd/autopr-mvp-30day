## CTO Objections & Responses

**Objection 1: "We already use GitHub Copilot / Cursor."**
> Copilot generates. It doesn't learn. Every suggestion is a fresh LLM call. We capture outcomes, verify against merge/close, and build reusable policies. The second time a bug appears, Copilot charges you again. We charge near zero.

**Objection 2: "How is this different from a linter or CI check?"**
> Linters enforce static rules. We build dynamic rules from real outcomes. A linter can't learn that a specific dependency pattern broke CI on three repos last week. Our system can — and will auto-apply that knowledge.

**Objection 3: "LLM costs are negligible for us."**
> Today they are. As your team grows from 50 to 500 engineers, LLM calls scale linearly with headcount. Our cost curve flattens — knowledge reuse scales sub-linearly. The question is not what you pay today, but what you pay at 500 engineers.

**Objection 4: "We need human review anyway."**
> You should have it. We don't replace reviewers — we reduce the noise they have to review. Trivial bugs at 93.8% accuracy can be auto-approved. Your senior engineers focus on architecture, not formatting.

**Objection 5: "Security / compliance concerns with AI in our pipeline."**
> Every decision is traced with causal chain: event → classification → generation → safety → trust → verification → outcome. Full audit trail. Policies auto-deactivate if accuracy drops below 60%. No black box.

**Objection 6: "We'll build this internally."**
> You can. Most teams underestimate the truth calibration layer — it took us 20 tables, 3 feedback loops, and 15 metric definitions to get right. If your core competency is shipping product, not building knowledge infrastructure, you're better off buying.
